Strategic Collaboration Proposal

Karam Brand U.S. Market Entry & Expansion

Presented by Abdallah Al Abed, CEO – Al Abed Inc.

Executive Summary

  • Goal: Launch and grow Karam in the U.S. market
  • Why Now: High demand for quality Middle Eastern products
  • How: Local representation + flexible distribution = faster growth, lower risk

Market Opportunity

  • Growing U.S. demand for authentic Middle Eastern food
  • Competition: Legacy brands like Ziyad Brothers dominate shelf space
  • Success requires trust, visibility, and flexible delivery

Phase 1: U.S. Regional Office

  • FDA, customs, and regulatory support
  • Product demos, in-store tastings, and local promotions
  • Retailer and distributor relationship management
  • Live feedback on pricing, shelf space, and competitors

Why a Regional Office?

  • Retailers prefer local partners
  • Faster reaction to market trends
  • Strengthens brand presence and visibility

Phase 2: Warehousing & Distribution

  • Mixed pallet model for smaller buyers
  • U.S. warehouse stocked monthly or bi-monthly
  • Large distributors continue direct container imports

Mixed Pallet Model Benefits

  • Lower entry barriers for new buyers
  • Reach underserved markets
  • Improved pricing and margin control

Consignment Agreement

  • Zamzam retains inventory ownership until sale
  • Al Abed Inc. manages fulfillment and customer coordination
  • Commission-based, transparent partnership

Strategic Advantage

  • Trusted U.S. partner with infrastructure
  • Efficient, scalable go-to-market model
  • Real-time insight, flexibility, and responsiveness

Next Steps

  • Align on partnership terms
  • Initiate regulatory processes
  • Plan inventory and distribution
  • Launch brand awareness campaigns
